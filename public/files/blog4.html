Introduction¶

The aim of this project is to predict the energy consumption (EC) of Ro-Ro vessels based on sensor data collected from the Danish Ro-Ro passenger ship MS Smyril. The dataset contains information such as fuel density, fuel volume flow rate, and environmental conditions collected during voyages from February to April 2010. Predicting energy consumption is essential for optimizing fuel efficiency, reducing emissions, and improving vessel operation.

Window Interval:¶

  • For this project, no explicit window interval was applied since the goal was to predict daily energy consumption ( EC) directly from the available features.

Feature Selection¶

The target variable, Energy Consumption (EC), was calculated using: EC=(fuelDensityxfuelVolumeFlowRatex3600x24)/1000 (tons/day)¶
Features such as fuelDensity and fuelVolumeFlowRate were selected as input variables based on their correlation with EC.¶

fuelDensity: This represents the density of fuel (in kg/L) and is crucial in determining the energy consumption because it directly affects the energy content of the fuel being used.

fuelVolumeFlowRate: This represents the volume of fuel flowing through the system (in L/s). It directly impacts the rate at which energy is consumed.

  • Features: fuelDensity and fuelVolumeFlowRate.
  • Target: Energy Consumption (EC)

Modeling¶

Three machine learning models were applied: Linear Regression, Decision Tree, and Random Forest.

Using Linear Regresion Model for prediction¶

  • Chosen for its simplicity and effectiveness in modeling linear relationships
  • The model returned an R-squared = 1.000, indicating a perfect fit.
  • The residuals showed a narrow, symmetrical distribution concentrated around 0, suggesting that the model is highly accurate for this dataset
